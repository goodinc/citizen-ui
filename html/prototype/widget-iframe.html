  • Back when I was a kid, my mom was a teacher, and got to bring the classroom computer home over the summer. When I was only around 3 or 4, I would play a "game" on the computer that taught me "QWERTY" typing skills. The game would reward me every time I correctly typed "The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog" (even though I was still learning those words). Because this was happening at the same time that I was really ramping up my literacy skills, I learned how to write/type on the computer very young--and technology was inseparable from my imagination. I started typing out short stories and a novel (I think about wolves in Alaska?) when I was in second grade, because I could put anything I dreamt up right there on the screen--typed up as fast as I could think it!
